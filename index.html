About us

Children who lack stable home are vulnerable to a number of adverse outcomes. Major threats, such as poverty and hunger, may precede episodes of homelessness; others stem directly from living without a home. Homeless children are more likely than other children to have moderate to severe acute and chronic health problems. Other problems such as hyperactivity, inattention, and behavioral problems are prevalent among this group. A quarter or more of homeless children have witnessed violence, and more than half have problems with anxiety and depression. A number of factors contribute to homelessness among children and youth. The chief factors are mental illness, substance abuse, and lack of affordable housing. Our mission is to provide High-quality early childhood education programs to enrich learning and nurturing the environments of disadvantaged children and youth. Early childhood education will improve the early-life of disadvantaged children, which in turn boosts a variety of early-life skills and later-life achievements.
